**Stormcaller** is a "spell bow" class in Erenshor.

## Description

Bringing Archery to Erenshor, Stormcallers go to battle by harnessing the power of the winds and skies through their bows, and they bring devastating burst damage to your group.

The Stormcaller is the most 'involved' class to play in Erenshor to date, requiring precise timing and planning, or else they may find themselves temporarily limited on their ability to deliver damage. They deal damage with a combination of bow skills, such as  Envenomed Arrow as well as spells such as  Arcstorm. Their key mechanic is the ability to power up spells with  Imbued Arrow for increased damage with an instant cast time at the cost of 3x the cool down. They have additional unique mechanics of  Precision and  Trick Shot to instantly reset cool downs, potentially making their DPS some of the highest in the game, but greatly subject to RNG.

Stormcallers also have unprecedented abilities to allow for "solo" play for the challenge oriented players of Erenshor.

## Playstyle

Stormcallers should be considered casters that happen to use a bow. As the stormcaller grows in level, more and more of their damage will come from spells rather than bow skills. The key to utilizing this effectively is the  Imbued Arrow. The imbued arrow skill instantly casts whatever ability the stormcaller is casting at the cost of 3x the cooldown. This allows a stormcaller to react quickly and put out large amounts of burst damage. It also requires a significant amount of mana, making items with the  Mana Charge,  Mana Charge II, and  Mana Flood abilities invaluable as well as the  Sage's Trinket. At higher levels, abilities like  Envenomed Arrow and  Razor Tipped Arrow provide additional, passive damage without costing mana, making them an excellent addition to the stormcaller's rotation.

Due to the variance of cooldowns from the  Precision passive or the  Trick Shot and the use of  Imbued Arrow skill, the stormcaller does not have a "fixed" rotation.

Against the average enemy, the stormcaller will typically open with  Ball Lightning or  Arcstorm and immediately use  Imbued Arrow to insta-cast it. Depending on level, a follow up with either  Envenomed Arrow or  Flaming Arrow arrow is warranted, though the latter quickly falls off in usefulness. From there, the rotation will vary based upon several factors.

For bosses or stronger enemies,  Razor Tipped Arrow provides a substantial amount of damage as it ticks for 3% of the enemy's current health. This also means it is less useful on lower level enemies and is not worth wasting a cooldown.

Using a DoT such as  Static Invocation or  Ionize causes a large amount of damage at an affordable mana cost. Using  Imbued Arrow on either ability puts two stacks of the DoT on the enemy, though this makes the cooldown extremely long and should only be done cautiously or after the stormcaller has the  Precision passive or the  Trick Shot ability to reset the cooldown faster.

Area effect damage is provided by lightning spells such as  Major Lightning, but these will break crowd control, which should be taken into consideration. To provide crowd control, the stormcaller can use the  Skycall ability, but this ability generates a large amount of threat, particularly if it resonates, and can be resisted easily. For those reasons, it is recommended to only use it sparingly and in emergency situations.

Blunted Arrow or imbued  Jolt provide insta-cast ranged interrupts, which are useful for pulling or dealing with casters at range.

Execute can be used to trigger the  Precision skill and reset cooldowns if timed properly, but it only receives a damage boost below 10%, making it difficult to time. As the stormcaller focuses more into intelligence, the damage of  Execute will also suffer, making its use even more situational.

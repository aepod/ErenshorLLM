# Combat Mechanics

Combat in Erenshor is initiated by left-clicking a target and pressing Q to attack. Right-clicking NPCs shows difficulty hints before engaging. Players can assign group roles: Main Tank (keeps aggro), Main Assist (sets targets), Healing/Mana (supports), Crowd Control (debuffs), and Puller (initiates combat from range).

---

Experience scales with party size: solo 100%, two-player 60% each, three-player 50% each, four-player 40% each. Enemy respawn timers reduce proportionally with group size, up to 44.4% faster in a four-player party. This balances solo and group play.

---

Armor Class (AC) reduces incoming physical damage through miss chance. Only enemies in front of a shield-wielding Paladin have a chance of being blocked. Two-handed weapons provide a 70% DPS bonus for Paladins. Windblades gain health through lifesteal mechanics.

---

Crowd control abilities include Sleep (Arcanist), Tangle (Druid), and various stun effects. The Control Chant skill book (Arcanist) and Blunted Arrow (Stormcaller interrupt) provide additional control options. Reavers access fear effects and slows for tanking utility.

---

Boss NPCs have bonus XP values and unique attack patterns. Some bosses are quest-connected, with defeating them progressing quest lines. Boss loot tables define guaranteed, common, uncommon, rare, legendary, and ultra-rare drops. The most coveted items come from legendary and ultra-rare boss drops.

---

Death causes respawning at the last Wishing Well where the player bound their soul. Wishing Wells are found in safe zones throughout Erenshor. Players should bind at appropriate wells before entering dangerous content.

---

The Ascension system provides endgame progression beyond the level 35 cap. Players unlock Ascension Points for purchasing specialized Ascension Skills through the AscensionBar interface. Each class has a level 35 capstone ability that must be learned.

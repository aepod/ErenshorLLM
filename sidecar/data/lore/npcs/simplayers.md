# Simulated Players

Simulated Players (SimPlayers) are AI-controlled NPCs who share the world of Erenshor with the player. They can be recruited to parties, participate in the Auction House, and appear throughout zones and dungeons.

---

There are two types of SimPlayers. Fixed SimPlayers remain consistent across all player instances -- 13 unique characters with set classes and starting levels (level 2-12). Examples include Jethro (Arcanist), Brock (Windblade), and Eron (Paladin). Randomly Generated SimPlayers vary per playthrough with randomized classes, factions, and personalities -- over 170 unique name options exist.

---

Experience scales down with party size: solo grants 100%, while four-player parties grant 40% per player. Enemy respawn rates improve proportionally -- a four-player party sees 44.4% faster respawns. Players can assign group roles: Main Tank (keeps aggro), Main Assist (sets targets), Healing/Mana (supports), Crowd Control (debuffs), and Puller (initiates combat).

---

To recruit SimPlayers, use "/whisper [name] let's group!" or "/shout LFG!" for zone-wide requests. SimPlayers accept group invites only within 3 levels of the player. Shouting LFG yields approximately 20% response; naming specific players guarantees response. Greetings trigger 40-50% response rates.

---

SimPlayers remember interaction history, items traded, and past adventures. Their personality is expressed through chat behavior via speech patterns including quirks like typos, ALL CAPS, third-person speech, and varying chattiness levels. Higher relationship values from grouping, helping in combat, and friendly chat make SimPlayers more helpful.

---

SimPlayers learn new skills automatically upon reaching required levels, except max-level skill books requiring manual trades. Logging out generates a minimum of 6 hours of simulated activity -- SimPlayers continue advancing, trading, and gearing offline. Each SimPlayer binds to character slots, maintaining level parity.

---

Notable SimPlayers with high auction greed modifiers: Cyndara and Sparkles (2.0x, making 10x markup), Jethro (1.75x, making 8.75x markup). SimPlayers use activatable gear abilities regardless of equipment status, making utility items valuable for buffs and damage absorption.

# Faction & Political Landscape of Erenshor

> A political analyst's dossier on all known factions, their allegiances, rivalries, and influence across Erenshor. Compiled from field intelligence, intercepted correspondence, and direct observation.

---

## I. The Religious Factions

### The Solunarian Brotherhood

**Leader:** Valao the Holy | **Members:** Leyor M'Chante, Akaran Black | **Base:** The church in Port Azure

The Brotherhood descends from the first Paladins -- Reavers whom Soluna forcibly converted upon her cosmic arrival at the crater of Soluna's Landing circa 1552. They built Port Azure over the next century and a half and remain its spiritual backbone. The Paladin class draws power from Solunarian Magic of Day and Night. They currently seek the legendary Flameblade. Allied with The Torchbearers of Brax, The Blades of Vitheo, Fernalla's Children, and all three civic factions. Hostile toward The Children of Sivakaya, Eldoth Molorai, and Savannah Priel.

### The Torchbearers of Brax

**Members:** Braxon Nelmar, Cerbantias Flameward | **Base:** Port Azure Lighthouse

Preservers of Brax's memory. The Elder God co-created Amarion with Siva, then buried his own city of Braxonia under desert sands to contain the monolith's corruption and withdrew in grief after losing his children Vitheo and Fernalla. The Torchbearers tend the Lighthouse flame as a symbol of his enduring light and offer assistance to Arcanists. Same alliance and enemy network as the Solunarian Brotherhood.

### Fernalla's Children

**Members:** Orion Mycon, Fernallan Sisters Hailey, Hawking, and Tessa | **Base:** A shrine outside Port Azure's walls

Druids who honor Fernalla, the demigoddess Brax created in Siva's image. Fernalla represents abundance and life, fell fighting corrupted Sivakaya, and ascended to godhood. Her legacy endures at Fernalla's Revival Plains and the Blooming Sepulcher. The Druid class channels her nature magic. They seek the artifact The Great Willow's Branch. Notably, they count The Agents of The Wisp among their enemies despite both factions revering aspects of the original divine family.

### The Blades of Vitheo

**Members:** Grayleigh Hawke, Sven Feartines | **Base:** Port Azure

Duelists devoted to Vitheo, son of Brax and Siva, who wielded Water and Air and achieved godhood through sacrifice in the War of the Gods. His legacy is preserved at Vitheo's Watch and Vitheo's Rest. The Windblade class draws from his influence. Like Fernalla's Children, the Blades list The Agents of The Wisp as enemies -- a critical split from the Solunarian Brotherhood and Torchbearers, who do not.

### The Agents of The Wisp

**Leader:** Larviso Poltir | **Members:** Wyland Sercher | **Historical Founder:** Eldrin Shadowmire | **Base:** Loomingwood

The most ideologically distinctive faction. Eldrin Shadowmire broke from Sivakaya's corrupted followers and retreated to Loomingwood Forest, warding it against her presence. Brax gave Eldrin the Nighthollow Candle -- lit by the Elder God's own hand -- to illuminate Sivakaya's uncorrupted memories. The Wisp worships the original Siva and seeks to restore her. Members bear blue-tinged skin and are secretive, unwelcoming to outsiders but not hostile. Widely thought responsible for the rituals at Stowaway's Step. Enemies of the Children of Sivakaya, Eldoth Molorai, and Savannah Priel. The Blades of Vitheo and Fernalla's Children view them with suspicion, creating a theological fracture within the "good" alliance bloc.

### The Children of Sivakaya

**Base:** The Bone Pits | **Alignment:** Corrupted, antagonist

The primary enemy faction. A cult of necromancers and risen undead worshipping Sivakaya, the corrupted Goddess of War. Their forces span from Sivakayan Raiders in low-level zones to Templars, Voidmasters, and the Grand Arcanist of Sivakaya in the Jaws of Sivakaya dungeon. Their resurgence triggered Captain Kilkay's Decree and is the defining political crisis of the current era. Enemy to every major civic and religious faction.

## II. Military & Civic Factions

### The Azure Guard

**Leader:** Captain Kilkay | **Base:** Port Azure

The military force entrusted by Merosavilla's government to protect their interests in Erenshor. Captain Kilkay issued the Decree of Port Azure upon learning of the Sivakayan resurgence: close all ports, recall all soldiers, terminate protection of outlying settlements. Citizens were invited to shelter within the city walls, even in "inns, alleys, streets, or warehouses." Allied only with The Citizens of Port Azure. Enemies: Ripper DeMerth and The Children of Sivakaya.

### Ripper DeMerth's Faction

**Leader:** Ripper DeMerth | **Members:** Captain Ovid, Hector Yvey | **Base:** Ripper's Keep

An Azure Guard officer who refused Kilkay's decree to abandon outlying settlements. Ripper's Rebellion saw her forces clash with former comrades, then defeat a Sivakayan fortification near Loomingwood -- humiliating the Azure Guard, who had believed Sivakayan dark magic made them invincible. The people named the captured fortress Ripper's Keep. A tense stalemate persists: each faction holds its keep, seeking to eliminate the other's leader. The player can pursue bounties on both leaders. Allied with The Citizens of Erenshor; hostile toward The Azure Guard and The Children of Sivakaya. Unaligned with the religious factions, making Ripper a wildcard.

### The Citizen Factions

**The Citizens of Port Azure** (leader: The King), **The Citizens of Erenshor** (leader: The King), and **The Citizens of Stowaway's Step** (notable: Controller Wendyl, Sergeant D'Vinn) represent Erenshor's civilian populations. They share identical alliances with the religious factions and the same enemy list. Stowaway's Step is the gateway island where all newcomers are redirected since passage to the mainland was blocked.

## III. Outlaw & Antagonist Factions

### Eldoth Molorai

**Leader:** Eldoth Molorai | **Members:** Overseer Orlok, Sagan Ilth, Molorai Captain Sigrid | **Bases:** Old Krakengard, Azynthi's Garden

A faction of elves who believe they are Erenshor's righteous protectors, working to prevent "marauders and interlopers" from gathering dangerous artifacts. They field a structured military: Militia Warriors, Arcanists, Archmages, Druids, Outriders, Veterans, Bloodwardens, Truthseekers, and an Eldermistress. They distribute a five-page manifesto and carry branded equipment. Whether they truly mean well remains ambiguous. Enemy of all alliance-bloc factions.

### Savannah Priel

**Leader:** Savannah Priel | **Members:** Priel Lieutenant Bovo, Addison Priel, Chazza Priel, Julianna Priel, Tiallia Priel | **Base:** Prielian Cascade

A bandit captain and Malaroth hunter operating from the Braxonian Desert. Her crew attempted to capture and export Malaroths to Merosavilla for profit. The organization fields Soldiers, Enforcers, Swiftblades, Deceivers, and Grimwatches. The learnable "Priel Strike" skill suggests their combat techniques have spread beyond their ranks. Enemy of nearly every major faction.

### The Necromancers of the Island Tomb

**Leader:** Stalzo | **Members:** Leah, Revid, Posta | **Base:** Island Tomb

A mysterious group whose fate depends on player choice. They reside in the Island Tomb on Stowaway's Step, allegedly containing a demon that prevents them from leaving. The player encounters them early and can collect their souls via Empty Soul Gems. Their connection to The Agents of The Wisp (also linked to Stowaway's Step rituals) is suggestive.

## IV. Non-Human & Mysterious Factions

**The Skyfolk** -- A fae race inhabiting Faerie's Brake (notable members: Tinkles, Winkles). Also found near the lake in Ripper's Keep. Largely uninvolved in human political conflicts.

**The Brackish Coast Nagaloks** -- Aquatic creatures encountered along the coast in varying maturity. The quest "Annaleigh's Nagalok Claws" (from Annaleigh Hephan) places them in conflict with adventurers.

**The Nightmare Collective** -- An entirely mysterious hostile faction occupying the Mysterious Portals, fielding Nightmarian Rakes, Arcanists, Knights, and Hounds. Connected to dream-themed enemies: Night Terror, Nightmare, Dream Invader, Waking Nightmare.

**The Huntsmen** -- The most obscure faction. Only one or two known members exist in the world. Purpose unknown.

**The Ancients** -- Referenced via the Arcane Headmaster, Arcane Pupil, and Ancient Educator. Possibly remnants of the Duskenlight school where Sivakaya once taught magic.

**The Followers of Good / Evil** -- Structural meta-factions in the game's code. The Followers of Good protects friendly NPCs from combat engagement; The Followers of Evil determines hostility. Both values persist in the player's save file.

## V. The Web of Alliances

**The Port Azure Alliance:** The Solunarian Brotherhood, Torchbearers of Brax, Blades of Vitheo, Fernalla's Children, Citizens of Port Azure, Citizens of Erenshor, Citizens of Stowaway's Step -- seven factions sharing virtually identical ally/enemy lists.

**Common Enemy List:** The Children of Sivakaya, Eldoth Molorai, Savannah Priel.

**Internal Fractures:** The Blades of Vitheo and Fernalla's Children both list The Agents of The Wisp as an enemy, while the Solunarian Brotherhood and Torchbearers do not -- a theological split within the alliance. The Azure Guard stands apart, allied only with Citizens of Port Azure and locked in stalemate with Ripper DeMerth, who is herself allied with the Citizens of Erenshor but unaligned with the religious factions.

## VI. Political Tensions in Port Azure

Port Azure hosts a layered power structure: the Merosavillan government holds formal authority through the Azure Guard, the Solunarian Brotherhood holds spiritual authority from the church, and the Torchbearers hold symbolic authority at the Lighthouse. Captain Kilkay's isolationist Decree contradicts the Brotherhood's mission to protect the faithful and the Torchbearers' legacy of confronting darkness. Ripper DeMerth's military victory against the Sivakayans proved Kilkay's position was folly. Yet neither faction can afford open war while the Sivakayan threat persists. Commerce adds another power layer: bankers Prestigio Valusha, Validus Greencent, and Comstock Retalio control finance; Thella Steepleton and Goldie Retalio manage the auction house. Port Azure is fortress, marketplace, cathedral, and political arena simultaneously.

## VII. The Friends' Club

Friends' Club is the rival guild of Erenshor -- an elite group composed exclusively of Rival SimPlayers. All Rivals are automatically placed into Friends' Club and cannot leave. They travel in optimized 5-person groups (tank, healer, crowd-control, two DPS) and respond to world events with competitive taunts. Their guild score advances at triple the normal rate (60% chance per check versus 20%), ensuring they dominate the leaderboard. Members have elevated greed values (3.0-4.0), refuse to group with outsiders, and use condescending dialog: "Oh, YOU'RE going to try that? Good luck with that, we cleared it weeks ago." Among themselves: "Nice pull, classic FC execution." They are entertaining antagonists, not villains -- the rival guild everyone loves to hate.

Other guilds: Erenshor's Young (default starter guild, SimPlayers below level 10, eager newbie chatter), Hatty Cats (cat-themed mid-tier guild with a "Meow" conversation topic), and various template guilds. Players can create up to four guilds. The top-scoring guild earns bonus loot drop chance.

## VIII. Faction Reputation and Gameplay

Faction reputation is a floating-point value per character, modified when completing quests or killing faction-aligned NPCs. The game displays "You've gained/lost standing with [faction]!" The critical threshold is zero: when a faction's value drops below 0, its NPCs become hostile and attack on sight. Hostile faction NPCs display red arrows on the world map. Values persist in the save file across sessions and zone transitions. A player who destroys their standing with a faction risks permanent lockout from its vendors, quest-givers, and safe zones.

## IX. Faction Founding Legends

Every major faction in Erenshor is a response to one catastrophe. Siva and Brax created Amarion together, burying a balancing darkness in the planet's crust. The gifted student Azynthi traveled north, returned corrupted, and manipulated Siva into a forbidden ritual that stripped her essence and birthed the monolith of corruption. Siva became Sivakaya.

The Torchbearers carry Brax's flame because he destroyed his own city to contain the blight. Fernalla's Children honor his daughter who died fighting her corrupted mother. The Blades of Vitheo remember his son for the same sacrifice. The Agents of The Wisp split from Sivakaya's followers because Eldrin Shadowmire refused to worship the corrupted goddess. The Children of Sivakaya embrace the corruption itself. The Solunarian Brotherhood exists because Soluna arrived centuries later and converted the Reavers into Paladins. The Azure Guard exists because the Solunarians built Port Azure. Every faction is, in the end, an answer to the question the monolith posed to the world: what do you do when a god falls?

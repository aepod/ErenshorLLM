# A Scholarly Chronicle of Erenshor: From Divine Creation to the Modern Age

*Compiled by the archivists of Port Azure, drawing upon the canonical texts "Godless Amarion," "Wardens of the Northern Veil," "The Construction of Port Azure," "The Fall of Rockshade Hold," the journals of Benjamin and Savannah Priel, and surviving Siva-Braxon Teachings recovered from the ruins of Duskenlight. Cross-referenced with oral traditions preserved by the Torchbearers of Brax, the Agents of the Wisp, the Solunarian Brotherhood, and the scattered Fernallan druids. Where accounts conflict, all versions are noted, for truth in Erenshor has always worn more than one face.*

---

## I. The Divine Creation: Siva, Brax, and the Shaping of Amarion

In the time before time, the Elder Gods Siva and Brax loved one another, and from that love they shaped the world of Amarion. Together they raised continents from the deep -- Merosavilla in the west and Erenshor in the east -- sculpting coastlines and mountain ranges, filling valleys with rivers and meadows with wildflowers. Siva personally crafted much of Erenshor's landscape: the rolling fields that would become Fernalla's Revival Plains, the ancient forests of Duskenlight and the Brake, the soaring peaks of the Sivakayan Spine, and the wind-swept expanses of Silkengrass Meadow.

Yet even gods must reckon with balance. As Siva and Brax fashioned Amarion's surface for light and prosperity, they buried a great darkness deep within the planet's crust -- a balancing darkness, necessary to the world's equilibrium. The obscured north of Erenshor, where this darkness pressed closest to the surface, remained uninhabited. Whether by divine intention or by instinct, humanity never settled there. The Twin Soldiers -- a pair of forbidding mountain peaks marking the boundary of the northern country -- stood as silent sentinels, warning all who approached that the lands beyond were not meant for mortal feet.

Humanity flourished in the south. Across both Erenshor and Merosavilla, peoples built settlements, farmed the land, and began to organize into cultures and communities. The Elder Gods walked among their creation, visible and present, and the world knew an age of unbroken hope.

## II. The Siva-Braxon School of Magic

In time, Siva and Brax established the Siva-Braxon School of Magic on the Duskenlight Coast, where the ancient forests met the sea. This was the place where magic was first freely given to the world. Within hallowed stone halls and along ancient stone walkways, Siva herself taught the elemental and arcane arts to mortal students, training the first Arcanists.

The school's teachings drew from the dual nature of its founders. Brax, who commands unrivaled power over all elements, brought the raw force of creation -- earth and fire, the shaping of matter, the binding of energy into form. Siva brought the subtler arts -- arcane knowledge, the manipulation of mana, the understanding of forces beyond the physical. Together their curriculum encompassed what would later fragment into the distinct magical traditions of Erenshor: elemental magic, arcane sorcery, nature magic, and the celestial arts that would not arrive until much later.

The Siva-Braxon Teachings, fragments of which survive today in the ruins along the Duskenlight Coast, speak of magic as a divine inheritance -- power flowing from the gods through the fabric of Amarion itself, accessible to any mortal with sufficient talent and discipline. This understanding would prove both the school's greatest gift and, through one student in particular, the instrument of the world's undoing.

## III. The Pantheon of Erenshor

### Siva -- The Lost Goddess of Arcane Knowledge

Before her fall, Siva was the more intellectually curious of the two Elder Gods. She delighted in sharing knowledge, in watching mortal minds grasp concepts that bridged the gap between mundane and divine. She created the demigod Vitheo, imbuing him with powers of Water and Air and binding him with an oath to protect the land in her name. She secretly guided chosen mortals to the northern reaches to serve as guardians of the Void -- the people who would become the Reavers.

Siva's domain encompassed arcane magic and forbidden knowledge. She understood the darkness beneath Amarion's crust better than Brax, for she was the one who had placed sentinels to watch over it. Scholars debate whether this familiarity was itself a vulnerability -- whether her intimate knowledge of the darkness made her susceptible to Azynthi's manipulation, or whether her willingness to accept forbidden power was born from a genuine vision of threat that Brax could not perceive.

Her corruption into Sivakaya remains the central tragedy of Erenshor's history. The Torchbearers of Brax speak of it as a betrayal -- Siva breaking her word to Brax. The Agents of the Wisp speak of it as a sacrifice -- Siva accepting what she believed necessary to save her people, deceived by one she trusted. The Children of Sivakaya do not speak of a fall at all, but of an ascension.

### Brax -- God of Earth, Elements, and Civilization

Brax is the builder. Where Siva delighted in knowledge, Brax delighted in creation. He raised the mountains of Erenshor, laid the foundations of Braxonia -- his great city -- and concerned himself with the sustenance and well-being of the land. He created the demigoddess Fernalla in the image of Siva, fashioning her to represent abundance, life, and fertility, ensuring that the world would prosper even when the gods turned their attention elsewhere.

Brax commands unrivaled power over all elements and is thought to have single-handedly brought magic to Erenshor in its practical, applied form. The Arcanists who follow him study his history and his writings to unlock new spells and magical abilities. The Torchbearers of Brax -- a brotherhood that tends the flame at the Port Azure Lighthouse -- preserve his memory and his teachings. Notable among them are Braxon Nelmar and Cerbantias Flameward, who maintain the sacred flame and offer assistance to Arcanists due to their common devotion.

Brax's story after Siva's corruption is one of escalating grief. He tried to warn Siva away from Azynthi's influence. He failed. He watched his lover transform into an abomination. He buried his own city and people beneath desert sands to stop the corruption from spreading. He gave the Nighthollow Candle to Eldrin Shadowmire in a last act of hope. He watched his children die in the War of the Gods. And then, broken by loss upon loss, he turned away from Amarion entirely.

The followers of Brax have a united purpose to this day: uncovering what ultimately happened to him. His origins are not known nor mentioned in any scripture. He appeared as suddenly as he departed. This mystery -- an Elder God whose beginning and ending are both unknown -- sits at the heart of Braxonian theology.

### Fernalla -- Demigoddess of Life, Fertility, and Nature

Fernalla was created by Brax in the image of Siva, though whether this was an act of devotion or an attempt to preserve something of his lover that might one day be lost, no text records. Her domain was abundance and the natural world -- life, death, disease, and healing, the full cycle accepted as truth by those who follow her. She is Erenshor's most studied and familiar deity, for her role in the Sivakayan war is well documented by the druids native to the continent.

Fernalla's power stood in direct opposition to the Blight. Where Sivakaya's corruption twisted and destroyed, Fernalla's influence restored and renewed. The Druids who channel her nature magic embrace all things natural, including death -- a fact that causes Fernalla's followers to be sometimes shunned by wider society. The great willow of Fernalla's Revival Plains, beneath which lies the Blooming Sepulcher, marks the place where her power is felt most strongly.

Fernalla fell in battle against the Sivakayan hordes during the War of the Gods. Her ascension to true godhood was witnessed by many, and she is commonly depicted in angelic form because of this. Her faction, Fernalla's Children -- led in part by the druid Orion Mycon and based near a shrine outside Port Azure -- searches to this day for a powerful artifact known as the Great Willow's Branch.

### Vitheo -- Demigod of Water and Air

Vitheo was created by Siva herself, imbued with the elemental forces of water and air. Upon his creation, he swore an oath to protect the land in Siva's name -- an oath that would torment him when his own mother became the land's greatest threat.

A statue of Vitheo stands on Stowaway's Step, greeting all newcomers to Erenshor's waters. His legacy is preserved at Vitheo's Watch, a southern coastal zone where his influence is still felt, and at Vitheo's Rest, a dungeon beneath Port Azure that bears his name. The Blades of Vitheo -- a faction of Duelists based in Port Azure, with notable members Grayleigh Hawke and Sven Feartines -- carry his martial tradition forward. The Windblade class draws power from his elemental influence, channeling the fury of wind and wave into combat.

Like Fernalla, Vitheo abandoned his mother Sivakaya when he saw what she had become, fighting alongside his father Brax. He fell through battle or treachery during the War of the Gods, and his death -- like Fernalla's -- elevated him to true divinity. The elemental forces of water and air that he embodied persist as a neutral force in Erenshor's magical ecology, belonging to neither the creative camp of Brax and Fernalla nor the destructive camp of Sivakaya and Azynthi.

### Sivakaya -- The Corrupted Goddess

What Siva became after Azynthi's ritual is something categorically different from what she was before. Sivakaya is described in the texts as "an unholy winged agent of death" -- a twisted, demonic figure in whom no semblance of Siva's original form remains. She is the Goddess of War in the eyes of her followers, and the embodiment of everything that went wrong with the world in the eyes of everyone else.

The Children of Sivakaya are a faction of necromancers, risen undead, and perhaps something else entirely, headquartered in the Bone Pits. They embrace her corrupted power, led by enigmatic figures including An Apparition of Sivakaya herself. Sivakayan forces are found throughout Erenshor -- raiders in low-level zones, soldiers and templars in endgame dungeons, and the dread Sivakayan Spectres that can manifest across the continent when observed through a Watcher's Lens.

The Jaws of Sivakaya, a dungeon hidden within the giant skull landmark on the Duskenlight Coast, and the shadowy monolith that still emanates corruption in the Blight are physical manifestations of her curse. The old church in Blacksalt Strand, where the Chapters of Descent can be found, speaks to the theological dimension of her following -- the belief among Sivakayans that their dark transformation was purposeful, even holy.

After the War of the Gods, Sivakaya was repelled beyond the Twin Soldiers into the north. For centuries she was silent. Now, credible sightings of Sivakayan soldiers have been reported in the forests and swamps of Erenshor, prompting Port Azure to close its gates and issue the Decree of Port Azure -- a state of martial emergency that defines the modern era.

### Azynthi -- The Corruptor of Gods

Azynthi's story begins as a mortal tale. He was a magical prodigy, the star pupil of the Siva-Braxon School, whose extraordinary talent Siva herself recognized from the moment he set foot upon the school's ancient stone walkways. His eyes flowed with an arcane gift; the world seemed to bend to his very thoughts as he passed through the hallowed halls.

Upon completing his training, Azynthi set off to explore the northern country beyond the Twin Soldiers, seeking knowledge that the school could not provide. What happened there is the great unanswered question of Erenshor's history.

The Reavers' account, preserved in "Wardens of the Northern Veil," provides the most detailed record. Azynthi arrived among the northern guardians claiming the blessing of Sivakaya herself. He learned quickly but lacked patience. The Void's offerings -- which the Reavers had learned to accept in measure, never refusing and never demanding more -- frustrated him. He wanted more than it would give. One night, he "stepped through a door that would never open again." His body remained on Erenshor, but its insides were twisted and wrong. He was what the Reavers had been posted to destroy. They failed -- the Void had magnified his natural power beyond anything they could restrain, and he departed their city, never to return.

When Azynthi reappeared in the south, he was markedly changed: quiet and pensive, lurking at the periphery of society, his eyes no longer bright with arcane gift but deadened and muted as though some unseeable shroud had been pulled across them. He retreated to a small island east of Braxonia -- now known as Azynthi's Garden, described as "the home of the Corruptor of Gods."

Whether Azynthi was himself a victim -- corrupted by the Void against his will, transformed into an instrument of the darkness beneath Amarion's crust -- or whether his ambition drove him willingly into forbidden power, remains debated. The Torchbearers regard him as pure evil, a manipulator who preyed on Siva's love for her people. The stone men of Rockshade Hold, in their fading lucidity, scratched his name into their walls with desperate reverence -- "hope, praise, sacrifice" -- suggesting that even among the cursed, some believe Azynthi holds the key to salvation. Scholars led by the brave Wishlan the Savior traveled east to seek his aid, only to be met with silence.

His island, Azynthi's Garden, now contains a Dimensional Rift and is inhabited by Azynthian creatures of fire and ice -- the Greater Azynthian Icebone and Greater Azynthian Firebone -- as well as Acolytes of Azynthi who guard stolen memories. The fire-and-ice duality of his domain hints at the convergence of Braxonian elemental power (which he may have drained from Siva) with the cold darkness of the Void.

### Soluna -- The Celestial Goddess

Soluna is not native to Amarion. She arrived from beyond the stars -- "fell from the stars," in some accounts -- landing with such force that she created a great crater in northern Erenshor, now known as Soluna's Landing. Her arrival was catastrophic: the sky caught fire, a mass of stone the size of a mountain was flung into the skies, and debris rained down in fire and ruin for days.

Her physical presence was immense. Those who stood alongside her described it as "standing within the heavens," sensing the presence of knowledge that would not fit within mortal minds. She was accompanied by elemental servants called the Solunarians, led by Valao the Holy, and she spread celestial teachings of Day and Night to the inhabitants of Erenshor.

But Soluna was not benevolent in the way Siva and Brax had been. When she encountered the Reavers -- northern guardians still carrying traces of divine and Void power -- she forcibly reshaped them, tearing the Void from their bodies and replacing it with her own stellar power. She demanded they claim the world in her name. They refused. She answered with anger, driving them into the mountains.

Those Reavers weak enough to listen to her nightly calls -- promises of love and mercy carried through the stars -- wandered toward her light and were claimed. These became the foundation of the Paladin order, carrying Soluna's doctrine across the land. The Solunarian Brotherhood, based in the church at Port Azure and led still by the legacy of Valao the Holy -- with notable members Leyor M'Chante and Akaran Black -- maintains her teachings to this day. They search for the legendary weapon Flameblade.

Soluna's departure is as mysterious as her arrival. The celestial entity Astra pursued her to Erenshor, and the heavens burned with their war until Soluna withdrew. Whether Astra was enemy, rival, or something else entirely is unclear. When Astra was defeated, she scattered into stardust at the crater's edge -- but the despair of the trapped Reavers fed her rebirth, and any who left the mountains were "drawn into her, consumed to restore what she had lost."

The deepest question surrounding Soluna is this: are the gods of Erenshor truly divine, or are they merely immensely powerful celestial beings? Siva and Brax created a world, but their origins are unknown. Soluna arrived from the stars with godlike power but behaved with very mortal anger and territorial ambition. The Reavers' account suggests that what mortals call "divinity" may simply be power beyond human comprehension -- and that the difference between a god and a very powerful being is, perhaps, no difference at all.

## IV. The Corruption of Siva and the Birth of the Blight

The chain of events that shattered the age of the Elder Gods began with a vision. Siva experienced painful premonitions of darkness and death spreading throughout Erenshor -- a creeping horror beyond the Twin Soldiers, composed of buried secrets and torment, a blight of sorrow and despair, and hordes of terrible devil-things walking amongst the shadows. Whether this vision was genuine prophecy, a manipulation seeded by Azynthi's Void-touched influence, or Siva sensing the very corruption that Azynthi himself would bring, is the cruelest irony of Erenshor's history.

She sought Azynthi's counsel. He spoke in hushed tones, sharing secrets unknown even to the Elder Gods. He promised her the means to repel any force that might threaten her beloved people. Brax tried to dissuade her, warning of a darkness within Azynthi that absorbed even the brightest flame. Siva promised Brax she would not accept the gift. She broke her word.

The ritual was performed in secret on the outskirts of Braxonia. As lights and bolts swirled around the pair, a cold northern wind swept across the field, blowing away all sensation of warmth. Sivakaya felt herself shrink deep within her own body, overwhelmed by encroaching darkness. Then, with a flourish of unknown magic, Azynthi tore the darkness back from her -- and with it, the very essence of Siva herself. He left her an empty husk, stripped of compassion, love, empathy, and agency. What remained was Sivakaya.

The sheer energy of the ritual brought into being a shadowy monolith -- a physical manifestation of the corruption. It leaked upon the land like flowing mud, creeping across hills and homes. Animals and people grew mad, violent, uncontrollable. Citizens fled or were overtaken by an incurable madness and a desire for destruction. This spreading corruption is the Blight -- visible today in the zone that bears its name east of the Braxonian Desert, where the land itself is twisted and corrupted terrain stretches toward Azynthi's Garden.

## V. The War of the Gods

When Sivakaya and her followers re-emerged from decades of seclusion, they were unrecognizable. Twisted, demonic figures driven by greed and selfishness, they nonetheless believed themselves heroes -- Sivakaya had foreseen a threat, and they were preparing to meet it, destroying everything in their path in demented fervor. Some factions worshipped her in darkened churches guarded by demonic figures. Others donned black armor and roamed the land as soldiers of a dark crusade.

War erupted between Brax's followers and the corrupted Sivakayans. Vitheo and Fernalla -- the children of both gods, forced to choose between their parents -- abandoned their mother and rushed to their father's side. The conflict raged for years across Erenshor, a scouring war that reshaped the continent.

Meanwhile, Eldrin Shadowmire and the loyal Sivakayans who had not been present for their goddess's corruption looked upon her new form with horror. They pulled away, retreated into Loomingwood Forest, and founded the Wisp -- a secretive society dedicated to researching ways to restore Sivakaya to her former self. They warded Loomingwood against Sivakaya's presence and created the Nighthollow Candle, a magical artifact. Brax blessed it with his own flame, giving it the power to illuminate past memories of Siva's uncorrupted self. To this day, the Agents of the Wisp -- led by Larviso Poltir, with notable members including Wyland Sercher -- operate from Loomingwood, their blue-tinged skin marking them as people who have spent too long in proximity to the Candle's strange light.

The war culminated in a Braxonian victory. Sivakaya was repelled to the north, beyond the Twin Soldiers. But Brax paid an unthinkable price: both Fernalla and Vitheo were felled through battle or treachery. Their deaths granted them true godhood -- Fernalla's ascension was witnessed by many, and Vitheo's sacrifice echoed through the elements -- but Brax would never again embrace his children.

## VI. Brax's Despair and the Braxonian Desert

To stop the blighted monolith from spreading further corruption, Brax tore down the very mountains and buried all of Braxonia beneath ashes and sand, forming a vast desert. His glorious city -- where the ritual had been performed, where his people had thrived -- was entombed. The sacrifice saved Erenshor from certain doom, but it destroyed Brax's own legacy in the process.

Beneath the sands, Fallen Braxonia endures as a dungeon -- the buried ruins of a civilization that was great before its god destroyed it out of love. Braxonian artifacts, game boards, pottery shards, and lost poems still surface in the desert, carried up by wind or uncovered by treasure hunters. The Braxonian people who survived the burial carried severe mental deterioration, described in contemporaneous accounts as attributed to "an unspecified historical event" -- the trauma of having their world literally collapse upon them.

After the war's end, Brax had become angry and distant. The world he and Siva had created as a beacon of light and hope was eclipsed by Sivakaya's embodiment of darkness. His people lay buried beneath the sands. His children were dead. His lover was a monster. Unable to bear further sight of their crumbling shared creation, Brax turned away, leaving the now godless land to its fate. The Braxon Brotherhood, traceable to the days of the great war, remains on Erenshor with incomplete records -- many documents lost or destroyed during the fighting.

## VII. The Wisp and the Nighthollow Candle

The Agents of the Wisp occupy a unique theological position in Erenshor. They are neither enemies of Sivakaya (as the Torchbearers and Solunarians are) nor her devotees (as the Children of Sivakaya are). They are followers of Siva -- the original goddess, uncorrupted -- and they believe she can be restored.

Their headquarters in Loomingwood Forest is warded against Sivakaya's presence, creating a sanctuary where the distinction between Siva and Sivakaya can be maintained. The Nighthollow Candle -- lit by Brax's own flame -- is their most sacred artifact, capable of revealing past memories of the goddess as she truly was. Whether these memories can be used to reverse the corruption remains the Wisp's central research question and their reason for existing.

The Wisp is widely thought to be responsible for the rituals on Stowaway's Step, though their motives in that regard are not known. They are hostile to Eldoth Molorai, Savannah Priel, and the Children of Sivakaya, but they maintain an uneasy neutrality with most other factions. Notably, the Blades of Vitheo count the Wisp among their enemies -- perhaps because Vitheo's followers see Siva's restoration as impossible or undesirable, given that their patron god died fighting what Siva became.

## VIII. The Arrival of Soluna and the Fall of Rockshade Hold

Nearly five centuries passed between Brax's departure and Soluna's arrival. The Reavers continued their silent vigil in the north. Erenshor was, in this era, truly godless -- abandoned by Brax, with Sivakaya lurking beyond the Twin Soldiers, Vitheo and Fernalla dead-become-divine but unreachable.

Then the sky caught fire. A mass of stone the size of a mountain was flung into the skies and fell southward for days, raining fire and ruin across the continent. The Reavers set out to investigate. What they found was Soluna.

The collateral damage of her arrival was devastating. Rockshade Hold, once a haven of silent scholars devoted to chronicling the origins of Erenshor and the divine bond between Brax and Siva, absorbed an overwhelming surge of ethereal material from the impact. The monks' flesh, unprepared for such concentrated magic, succumbed. Skin turned to stone. Joints stiffened. Bodies became entombed in otherworldly rock. The lucky ones died in the fiery chasms birthed by the impact. The survivors were cursed with unaging immortality as living stone -- unable to write, learn, or reason, monuments to the history they had so diligently studied.

From the magical onslaught, a great flame elemental called Pyroclast was born and enslaved the cursed population. Upon their molten master's orders, the stone men became unwilling hunters, attacking all living creatures on sight, spreading their unending rage and pain. In their despair, some scratched the name "Azynthi" into their walls with desperate reverence. A brave group led by Wishlan the Savior escaped east to seek Azynthi's aid, but were met only with silence.

## IX. The Modern Era: Port Azure and the Present Day

### The Founding of Port Azure (Year 1552)

After Soluna's departure -- driven away by Astra's celestial war -- the Solunarian Paladins she left behind discovered Soluna's Landing and recognized it as a site of extraordinary spiritual significance. They launched an initiative to encourage the other peoples of Erenshor to join them in creating a capital city.

The response was remarkable. The Fernallans, Braxonians, and Vitheans -- peoples who had spent centuries in scattered, diminished communities -- came together, drawn by the belief that their gods had been physically present on Erenshor. Construction commenced in 1552 and was formally completed in 1700, a milestone marked by widespread commemoration.

During early construction, a section of the castle suffered structural collapse, destroying what some believed to be a Sivakayan prison and interrogation center operated by the Azure Guard. The remains became the Lost Cellar -- a dungeon that persists beneath Port Azure to this day. Whether Sivakayan operatives were truly being held there, at a time when Sivakayans as a tangible threat were not widely accepted, remains unresolved.

### Governance and the Azure Guard

The Government of Merosavilla assumed administrative control of the city, owing to their role in provisioning the majority of settlers. Under their authority, the Azure Guard was established to enforce order with an iron fist. Captain Kilkay commands the Guard, a staunch believer that the return of Sivakayans to Erenshor demands total consolidation of forces behind Port Azure's walls.

Kilkay's Decree of Port Azure closed the ports to all travelers, recalled all soldiers to the city garrison, terminated protection of outlying settlements, and warned citizens that remaining outside the walls would be at their own peril. This decree -- a response to credible intelligence of awakened Sivakayans -- defines the current political crisis.

### Ripper's Rebellion

Not all agreed with Kilkay's strategy of retreat. Ripper DeMerth, who had commanded the Azure Guard forces responsible for protecting settlements throughout Erenshor, refused the order to abandon her people. Her defection led to Ripper's Rebellion -- armed clashes between her forces and their former comrades until neither side could achieve decisive victory.

Ripper's soldiers then defeated Sivakayan forces occupying a fortification near Loomingwood and established it as their headquarters. The people of Erenshor began to call it Ripper's Keep -- a humiliation for the Azure Guard, who had believed that fighting the Sivakayans directly was pure folly. An uncomfortable stalemate persists, with each faction in their respective keeps, each seeking a gap in the other's armor.

### The Factions of Modern Erenshor

Port Azure sits at the nexus of competing interests. The Solunarian Brotherhood, the Torchbearers of Brax, Fernalla's Children, and the Blades of Vitheo are allied with the Citizens of Port Azure against the Children of Sivakaya, Eldoth Molorai, and Savannah Priel's bandits. The Eldoth Molorai -- a faction of elves based in Old Krakengard and Azynthi's Garden -- believe themselves the righteous protectors of Erenshor, guarding powerful artifacts from marauders and interlopers. Whether they truly mean well or serve darker purposes remains unknown. The Skyfolk -- a race of Fae centered in Faerie's Brake -- remain neutral, as do the Brackish Coast Nagaloks and the enigmatic Necromancers of the Island Tomb, who keep a demon contained on Stowaway's Step. The Nightmare Collective, entirely mysterious and hostile, occupies the strange portals that have appeared across the land.

## X. Elemental and Domain Interconnections

The magical ecology of Erenshor reflects the relationships among its deities. Earth and elemental mastery flow from Brax, manifesting in the Arcanist tradition and the raw power preserved by the Torchbearers. Life, death, and the natural cycle belong to Fernalla, channeled through Druidic magic. Water and Air are Vitheo's legacy, powering the Windblade fighting style and the elemental combat of the Blades of Vitheo. Celestial magic of Day and Night derives from Soluna, expressed through Paladin abilities and the Solunarian Brotherhood's teachings.

Arrayed against these are the forces of corruption. Sivakaya's domain is war, death, and the twisting of living things. Azynthi commands the Void -- a dimension of darkness that bleeds through unseen seams, connected to the north and to corruption. The Blight is the physical frontier where these forces clash: Fernalla's life-giving power pushes against Sivakaya's corruption in an ongoing struggle visible in the landscape itself.

The fire-and-ice duality of Azynthi's Garden -- Greater Azynthian Firebones and Icebones serving side by side -- suggests that Azynthi's stolen power combines Braxonian elemental mastery with the cold emptiness of the Void. This hybrid nature may explain why no single faction or magical tradition has been able to undo what Azynthi wrought.

The Reavers represent yet another tradition: mortals who learned to coexist with the Void through discipline rather than domination. Their transformation by Soluna into proto-Paladins was a violation -- the forcible replacement of one supernatural bond with another. That the Reavers resisted, and that some were consumed by Astra rather than submit to Soluna's reshaping, speaks to the contested nature of divine power in Amarion.

## XI. Mysteries and Open Questions

The history of Erenshor is riddled with gaps that no surviving text can fill, and it is in these gaps that the most provocative questions dwell.

**What did Azynthi find beyond the Twin Soldiers?** The Reavers describe him stepping through "a door that would never open again," his body remaining but its insides twisted and wrong. Was this a portal to the Void itself? A direct encounter with the balancing darkness that Siva and Brax had buried? Or something else entirely -- an intelligence, an entity, a truth so terrible that it reshaped the most gifted mortal mind on Erenshor into an instrument of divine destruction?

**Are the gods truly divine?** Siva and Brax are called Elder Gods, but their origins are unknown. Soluna arrived from the stars with godlike power but petty anger. The Reavers' account suggests that "divinity" may be a question of scale rather than kind -- that what mortals worship as gods are simply beings of such immense power that the distinction becomes academic. If Azynthi, a mortal, could strip an Elder Goddess of her essence, what does that say about the nature of godhood?

**Why did Soluna really leave?** The official account is that Astra pursued her and drove her away. But Astra scattered into stardust -- she was defeated, not Soluna. Why, then, did Soluna not return? Did she accomplish what she came for? Was Erenshor merely a waypoint in a larger celestial journey? Or did she see something in Amarion -- the Void, the buried darkness, the echoes of Siva's corruption -- that gave even a celestial goddess pause?

**What is the Monolith's true nature?** The monolith formed from the "sheer energy" of Azynthi's ritual. Brax buried it beneath the desert, but the Blight persists and spreads. Is the monolith destroyed, or merely contained? Is it a wound in reality, a doorway, or something with its own terrible will?

**Can Siva be restored?** The Wisp believes so. The Nighthollow Candle illuminates memories of what she was. But memories are not essence, and Azynthi took her essence into himself. To restore Siva would require reclaiming what Azynthi stole -- and Azynthi sits in his Garden, surrounded by creatures of fire and ice, guarding stolen memories, silent to all who seek him.

**What is Astra's nature and purpose?** She fought Soluna, was destroyed, was reborn from the despair of the trapped Reavers, and now consumes anyone who leaves the northern mountains. Is she a cosmic predator? A counterbalance to Soluna? A manifestation of the Void's response to celestial intrusion? The Reavers, sealed in their hills, have no answers -- only the certainty that this is the place where they are forever lost.

**What stirs in the north?** Sivakaya was repelled beyond the Twin Soldiers centuries ago. Now her soldiers are returning. The Decree of Port Azure responds to "new intelligence" of awakened Sivakayans. Is Sivakaya herself returning? Has she grown stronger in the north, feeding on the Void that corrupted Azynthi? Or is the current Sivakayan resurgence the work of something else entirely -- the creeping horror that Siva originally foresaw, the very threat that Azynthi used to manipulate her?

*Thus stands the chronicle of Erenshor in the present age: a continent born from divine love, shattered by mortal ambition, abandoned by its creators, and now caught between the iron walls of Port Azure and the gathering shadows beyond the Twin Soldiers. The gods are absent or corrupted, the factions are fractured, and the land itself bears scars that no amount of Fernallan magic or Solunarian prayer has been able to heal. Into this world come the adventurers -- arriving on Stowaway's Step, seeking passage to a continent that has closed its doors -- carrying with them the only resource that has ever truly mattered in Amarion: the stubborn refusal to accept that the story is over.*

# Resistance Gear Guide

Erenshor has four resistance types: Magic Resistance (MR), Poison Resistance (PR), Elemental Resistance (ER), and Void Resistance (VR). Resistances reduce incoming damage of that type. Many endgame bosses deal heavy elemental or magic damage, making resist gear the difference between a clean kill and a wipe.

---

## Resistance Types and When They Matter

**Magic Resistance (MR):** The most broadly useful resist. Many bosses and elite enemies cast spells. MR is relevant from mid-game onward and remains critical in endgame. Prioritize this on every class.

**Poison Resistance (PR):** Important in The Blight, Blooming Sepulcher, Rottenfoot, and against Sivakayan enemies that use poison abilities. Druids and tanks should carry PR gear for specific encounters.

**Elemental Resistance (ER):** Essential in Rockshade Hold (fire enemies, Volcanic Giant), Braxonian Desert (Monarch of the Flame), and Fallen Braxonia (Braxonian Tempest). Stormcallers are inherently exposed to elemental fights as a ranged class.

**Void Resistance (VR):** Niche but devastating when needed. Void damage appears in Vitheo's Rest, Azynthi's Garden, Jaws of Sivakaya, and against Sivakayan Voidmaster and Voidweaver enemies. Reavers and anyone farming endgame Sivakayan content should stack VR.

## Per-Class Resist Priorities

**Paladin (Tank):** MR first, then balanced across all four. Tanks eat every spell the boss throws. Thryne (MR +10%) and Eclipse aura are your best friends. Stack Terra Crown and Diamond Helm for head slot resists.

**Paladin (DPS):** MR and ER. Most bosses you farm (Monarch, Astra) deal elemental and magic damage. Molten Axe (ER +10%) doubles as your DPS weapon.

**Windblade:** MR and VR. You are in melee range and many endgame Sivakayan enemies deal void damage. Dreamborn Gloves (VR +3%) early, Oslind's Eviscerator (VR +3%, ER +3%) later.

**Arcanist:** MR above all else. You are fragile and magic damage will one-shot you before physical ever does. Tome of Necromancy (VR +12%) is unique for void encounters. Flawless Diamond Ring (MR +18%) is the single best resist item in the game for casters.

**Druid:** Balanced resists through Decay aura (all +10). Supplement with Forest Pendant (PR +3%) for poison-heavy zones. Frozen Mana Charm (MR +4%) is a strong neck slot pickup.

**Stormcaller:** ER priority since you are often fighting elemental enemies at range. Volcanic Sceptre (ER +15%) as a swap weapon for Rockshade Hold farming. Lightning Strike aura covers MR, ER, and PR at +10 each.

**Reaver:** VR and MR. Reavers live at low health for damage bonuses, making resist stacking a matter of life and death. Tome of Necromancy in off-hand for VR +12%. Bastion shield (MR +3%) for tank builds.

## Resist Stacking Strategy

For general endgame content, aim for at least MR +15%, with +5-8% in each other resist type. For specific encounters:

- **Rockshade Hold / Braxonian Desert fire bosses:** Stack ER +20%+ using Volcanic Sceptre, Molten Axe, Chunk of Living Stone aura, and Living Ruby Ring.
- **Vitheo's Rest / Azynthi's Garden void bosses:** Stack VR +10%+ using Tome of Necromancy, Ascended Remains, Azynthian Cape, and Solar Forged Ring.
- **The Blight / Blooming Sepulcher poison zones:** Stack PR +10%+ using Fungal Greatsword, Sivakayan Axe, Terra Crown, and Wild Colossus Ring.
- **General boss farming:** Aged Prism aura (+8 all resists party-wide) combined with class IV aura covers most situations.

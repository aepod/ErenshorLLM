# The Adventurer's Guide to Erenshor: Zones, Dungeons, and the Road to Power

*Written by a veteran of Port Azure who has walked every trail, cleared every dungeon, and lived to tell of it -- mostly.*

---

## The Shape of the World

Erenshor is a continent within the world of Amarion, shaped by the hands of the Elder Gods Sivakaya and Brax themselves. The land stretches from sun-warmed southern coasts to corruption-scarred northern wastes, with every zone between carrying the weight of divine conflict. Understanding the geography is not mere curiosity -- it is survival. The wars between Sivakaya and Brax, the arrival of Soluna from the stars, the treachery of the mortal sorcerer Azynthi -- all of these left scars on the land that you will walk through, fight in, and need to comprehend if you want to reach level 35 alive.

The southern coast is where new arrivals make landfall. The western seaboard climbs through forests and mountain passes into the Priel bandit strongholds. The central heartland rolls through revival plains, swamps, and vast grasslands. The east bakes under the Braxonian Desert before descending into The Blight. The north holds Soluna's crater and the contested fortress of Ripper's Keep. And far to the east, across dark waters, lies Azynthi's Garden -- the island where a mortal corrupted a goddess and changed the world forever.

Port Azure, the capital city on the southeastern coast, serves as your hub throughout. The Auction House, banks, trainers, and guild hall are all here. You will return often.

## Recommended Leveling Path

### Levels 1-8: The Southern Shore

**Stowaway's Step** (Level 1-6) is where most adventurers begin. You arrive at the Docks as a stowaway and work your way through the town of Wickliff and the surrounding wilds. The island is small but packed with fundamentals: A Brackish Croc Whelp near the docks, A Young Wolf and A Grass Spider on the hillsides, and Islander Bandits lurking in camps. Bosses here include Plaxitheris (a ghostly serpent) and Vevillo Polter. The **Island Tomb** (Level 1-8) is your first dungeon -- spirits and skeletons within, with the Ghost of Wyland Sercher as the main event. NPCs like Neziroth Daggerhart and Sergeant D'Vinn give starter quests, and Wally Waldorf will get you started on fishing.

*Farming tip: Island Tomb is excellent repeatable experience at levels 3-6. Clear the spirits and skeletons, reset, repeat.*

**Willowwatch Ridge** (Level 1-8) is the alternative start for experienced players -- no tutorial, tougher enemies from the jump. Corrupted Oreads, Juvenile Nagaloks, and Grassland Ogre Gatherers roam here alongside the usual wolves and spiders. Bosses like Autmis the Lost and Battlefist are noticeably harder than their Stowaway's Step counterparts. Exits lead to Fernalla's Revival Plains, The Bone Pits, and Underspine Hollow.

Once you outgrow Stowaway's Step, take the boat to the mainland. You have three early destinations:

**Hidden Hills** (Level 4-8) sits on the southern coast. Wolves, spiders, Brittle Skeletons, and Priel Bandit Recruits populate the area. Bosses Alpha Wolf and the Priel Lieutenants Bovo and Lance offer solid loot. The **Bone Pits** dungeon (Level 3-9) lies beneath the hills -- a skeleton-infested pit where Zashlyn Bloodbane and An Apparition of Sivakaya await. This is your first taste of Sivakayan corruption.

**Faerie's Brake** (Level 3-10) is a forested zone on the southern coast where faeries, bears, and wolves coexist uneasily. The faerie creatures -- A Faerie Drone in particular -- are the first caster-type enemies many players face. Grizzlepaw the bear is a sought-after boss for early leather drops. Tinkles, the rare faerie boss, drops prized items. Old legends speak of a demon of flesh and shadow haunting the swampy heart of these woods under pale moonlight -- the Beast of The Brake.

**Vitheo's Watch** (Level 4-10) hugs the southern coast and is defined by its connection to the sea god Vitheo. Brackish Crocs patrol the shore, Lost Sea Giants wade the shallows, and the Eldoth Molorai faction maintains a presence through their Militia forces. The Lost Sea Giant King is a formidable early boss. **Old Krakengard** (Level 8-13) is the dungeon here -- a Molorai stronghold where Overseer Orlok and Molorai Captain Sigrid command fanatics. Bring a group.

*Danger: The Molorai Militia in Vitheo's Watch will attack on sight. Do not assume all humanoid NPCs are friendly.*

### Levels 6-20: The Middle Lands

**Fernalla's Revival Plains** (Level 6-18) is the great central zone and a crossroads of the world. Named for the goddess Fernalla, child of Brax and Sivakaya who fell in the divine war and ascended to divinity, these plains are where life persists despite everything. Brown Bears, Grassland Ogres, Highwayman Raiders, and Rottenfoot Tribalists share the territory. The Sylvan Giant and Gruhglor are the zone bosses. The Spirit of the Willow watches over a great tree beneath which lies the **Blooming Sepulcher** -- a multi-level dungeon (Level 18-34) that intertwines with Vitheo's Rest in Port Azure. This questline is one of the longest in the game, requiring you to shuttle memories between the two dungeons to unlock deeper floors. At the bottom waits Fallen Fernalla herself.

**Underspine Hollow** (Level 10-16) is the ogre dungeon, accessible from the plains. Grassland Ogre Plaguebringers, Shamans, Thaumaturges, and Sorcerers fill these caves. Druo the Reborn always spawns; the other bosses -- Agsha of the Soil, Rosle of the Spirits, Ughl of the Skies, Obba of the Warkind -- rotate randomly.

*Farming tip: Underspine Hollow ogres drop Ogrespice Leaf and decent coin. At level 14-16 this is one of the best experience-per-hour spots in the game.*

**Duskenlight Coast** (Level 7-19) stretches along the western shore. This is where Sivakaya once taught magic freely at her school before her corruption. The giant skull landmark dominates the skyline -- inside it lies the entrance to the **Jaws of Sivakaya** (Level 30-35), an endgame dungeon you cannot enter yet. For now, fight Brown Bears, Nagaloks, Arcane Pupils, Ancient Educators, and Molorai Archaeologists. The Dusken Giant is a memorable boss -- the book "Strange Beasts of Erenshor" describes giant men in these forests who tread softly but leave imprints the size of shields.

**Rottenfoot** (Level 12-20) is a foul swamp north of Fernalla's Revival Plains. Rottenfoot Maulers, Witchdoctors, and Swampwalkers are the native goblinoid tribe. Nipper creatures infest the waterways. Sivakayan Clerics, Raiders, and Reavers operate here openly. Swamp Wyrm and Tek-Nu Dah are the standout bosses. The swamp connects to Elderstone Mines.

**Blacksalt Strand** (Level 14-21) lies just north of Port Azure. Grizzly Bears, Nagaloks, Seafiends, and Seaside Gorillas roam the salt-crusted beaches. The Sivakayan presence intensifies here: Acolytes and Prioresses conduct dark rituals. Lazalith, Soldier of Sivakaya is a dangerous boss. The Corpses of Brice and Cyndreela Mathers hint at a tragic questline. The **Elderstone Mines** (Level 16-21) dungeon is accessed from here -- Deeplings and Fungal creatures in the deep, with the Slumbering Queen and Ravager, The First Infected as bosses.

*Danger: Sivakayan enemies in Blacksalt Strand hit harder than the wildlife. If you see a Sivakayan Cleric healing other mobs, kill the cleric first.*

**Lost Cellar** (Level 13-20) is a dungeon beneath Port Azure itself -- the collapsed remains of what may have been a Sivakayan interrogation center, destroyed during Port Azure's construction in the 1500s. Spark Beetles, Spectral Guards, and Rotting Royal Hounds haunt its depths. The Spark Queen is the primary boss.

### Levels 15-30: The Broad World Opens

**Braxonian Desert** (Level 16-25) dominates the east. This wasteland was created when Brax himself smote the earth to destroy his own city and contain Sivakaya's spreading corruption. Desert Jackals, Sand Mummies, Scorched Skeletons, and Braxon Fanatics wander the dunes. The Sivakayan invasion force is active here -- Assassins, Bowmistresses, Grand Arcanists, and High Priestesses of Sivakaya appear as board-game-themed bosses alongside Candlekeeper, Kingsman, and Monarch of the Flame. Beneath the desert lies **Fallen Braxonia** (Level 28-33), the buried ruins of Brax's city, guarded by Braxonian Honor Guards and the boss duo Prince Oslind and Prince Ardant.

**Silkengrass Meadowlands** (Level 10-30) is the vast central grassland. Its enormous level range makes it relevant for a long time. Dire Wolves, Kodiaks, Giant Skeletons, Silk Cobras, and Sivakayan forces patrol here. The legendary Albino Kodiak is a rare spawn -- whispered to bring luck or doom depending on the intent of the observer. Morian the Ascended and the Sylvan Giant are the zone bosses. Ripper Soldiers recruiting in the meadows hint at the conflict to the north.

*Farming tip: Silkengrass at levels 22-28 offers steady experience from Dire Wolves and Sivakayan groups, with a chance at the Albino Kodiak rare.*

**Windwashed Pass** (Level 15-30) is the western mountain crossroads connecting Duskenlight Coast, Malaroth's Nesting Grounds, Silkengrass Meadowlands, and the Prielian Cascade. Seafiend Workers, Manathites, and Manalords cluster near water. Priel Deceivers and Enforcers patrol the trails. Stonemen -- monks turned to living rock by the shockwave of Soluna's arrival -- stand silent in the passes. **Rockshade Hold** (Level 22-26) is the Stoneman dungeon, a ruined monastery where Diamond Colossus, Pyroclast, and Stoneman Colossus await. The volcanic elements within suggest connections to Brax's elemental power.

**Loomingwood** (Level 18-26) is the great northern forest, home of The Wisp -- the faction led by Eldrin Shadowmire that broke from Sivakaya and now works to restore her uncorrupted self. The forest is warded against Sivakaya's presence. Loomingwood Minotaurs in soldier, legionnaire, and hero variants are the primary threats. Aetherfiends, Devoratrix, and Loomingwidows lurk in the canopy. Pteriaped Chicks and the rare Pteriaped Matron nest in the heights. The Gardener of Loomingwood and Wardwarped Alpha are notable bosses. This zone connects to Malaroth's Nesting Grounds and Ripper's Keep.

**Malaroth's Nesting Grounds** (Level 20-30) is where the fearsome Malaroths breed. These creatures are described in "The Malaroth Ledger" as fury wrapped in muscle, worth more gold than most people see in a lifetime. Malaroth Cubs and Mature Malaroths alongside Priel Tamers and Deceivers make this zone dangerous. Savannah Priel and her crew once attempted to capture and export Malaroths to Merosavilla -- you will meet members of her family in the Prielian Cascade. The Demented Malaroth, touched by Sivakaya's corruption, is terrifyingly strong. Feed a Moongill fish to a Malaroth at your own risk -- it attracts "a darker sort of beast."

### Levels 25-35: The Endgame Approach

**Soluna's Landing** (Level 25-33) is the crater in the north where the cosmic entity Soluna arrived from the stars, setting the sky on fire and flinging stone into the heavens. Celestial Matter, Living Meteorites, and Stardust creatures reflect the otherworldly nature of the impact. Ancient Corpses, Celestarians, Guards, and Knights from a civilization destroyed by the landing still walk here. Raval, The First Paladin -- a historical figure who submitted to Soluna and founded the Paladin order -- is a boss encounter. Astra, Rogue of the Stars, who warred with Soluna in the heavens and scattered into stardust at the crater's edge, also manifests here.

**The Blight** (Level 26-33) is Sivakaya's corruption made manifest in the eastern reaches. Blighted Jackals, Blight Demons, Doomwalkers, Lurkers, and Obsidian Horrors choke the land. Blighted Priests channel dark power. The Harbinger of Corruption, Fenton the Blighted, and the Blight Wyrm are formidable bosses. A Boatman at the docks offers ferry passage to Azynthi's Garden. The Stoneman Seekers here search for something lost.

*Danger: The Blight has no safe camps. Every direction holds aggressive enemies. Do not traverse alone below level 28.*

**Ripper's Keep** (Level 28-33) is an ancient Sivakayan fortification in the far north, recently seized by Ripper DeMerth and her soldiers. Sivakayan Clerics, Reavers, and Raiders still patrol outside the walls, while Faerie Foliagians, Windchildren, and Enchantresses occupy the surrounding forest. Ripper DeMerth herself is the zone boss. Merchants Goldie Retalio and Comstock Retalio operate inside the keep. **Abyssal Lake** (Level 24-33) is the dungeon here -- a dark underground lake reached through a cave on the eastern side of the keep, with a secret entrance behind the waterfall in Silkengrass Meadowlands. Sivakayan Templars, Faithbound, and Devoted guard the depths alongside Nipper creatures. The Nipper King and the Abyssal Lord are the final bosses.

**Azynthi's Garden** (Level 30-35) is the endgame island, accessed by ferry from The Blight. This is where the mortal sorcerer Azynthi traveled north seeking forbidden knowledge, returned changed, and eventually stripped Sivakaya of her divine essence in a catastrophic ritual. The Eldoth Molorai faction controls the island: Molorai Anointed, Truthseekers, Bloodwardens, Nightmistresses, and Acquirers are everywhere. Giant Watchdogs and Wild Ancestral Dogs roam freely. Sivakayan Bodyguards and Emissaries represent the corrupted goddess's lingering interest. The **Dimensional Rift** within the garden is a separate zone with Deadweb Spiders, Azynthian Firebones and Icebones, Faceless Necromancers, Ghouls, and Botanists. Oracle Tretha, Oracle Miress, Vos Ashlyn, Grounds Keeper, and the Exalted Firebone are rift bosses. Eldoth Molorai himself is the final encounter on the main island.

## Dungeon Guide: The Deep Places

### Early Dungeons (Level 1-16)

- **Island Tomb** (1-8, Stowaway's Step): Spirits and undead. Five bosses including Ghost of Wyland Sercher. Good for learning group dynamics. NPCs Leah, Posta, Revid, and Stalzo offer soul gem quests.
- **The Bone Pits** (3-9, Hidden Hills): Skeletons and Sivakayan agents. Zashlyn Bloodbane and An Apparition of Sivakaya. First exposure to Sivakayan corruption theme.
- **Old Krakengard** (8-13, Vitheo's Watch): Molorai stronghold. Overseer Orlok and Molorai Captain Sigrid command a garrison. Bring interrupts for the Archmages.
- **Underspine Hollow** (10-16, Fernalla's Revival Plains): Ogre caves. Druo the Reborn is guaranteed; four other bosses rotate randomly.

### Mid-Level Dungeons (Level 13-26)

- **Lost Cellar** (13-20, Port Azure): Spectral guards and spark beetles beneath the city. The Spark Queen drops decent caster gear.
- **Mysterious Portal** (14-35, multiple locations): Three versions unlocked by rituals at Duskenlight Coast (14-15), Fernalla's Revival Plains (21-24), and Ripper's Keep (28-35). Nightmare-themed enemies. Hypnic in the third portal is endgame-worthy.
- **Elderstone Mines** (16-21, Blacksalt Strand): Deeplings and fungal horrors. Ravager, The First Infected is a challenging boss with a fascinating lore connection -- this is where Sivakaya's corruption first took root in living flesh. The Slumbering Queen drops coveted items.
- **Rockshade Hold** (22-26, Windwashed Pass): A ruined monastery filled with Stonemen -- monks transformed to living rock when Soluna's arrival shattered the mountains. Diamond Colossus, Pyroclast, and Volcanic Giant. Fire and earth resistance help enormously.

### Endgame Dungeons (Level 22-35+)

- **Blooming Sepulcher** (18-34, Fernalla's Revival Plains): The longest dungeon questline in the game. Three tiers (Upper, The Pit, The Willow) unlocked by shuttling memories between here and Vitheo's Rest. Fallen Fernalla at the bottom is one of the hardest encounters. Fawn of Fernalla and Risen Hierophants populate the deepest level.
- **Vitheo's Rest** (22-35, Port Azure): Four wings. The Shrines (21-24) hold Balaste the Fallen. The Traitor's Halls (30-38) hold Nazura and Elwio the Traitor, plus The Abomination. Vitheo's Tomb (34-38) contains the Apparition of Vitheo. The hidden wing Under the Lighthouse (35+) holds Darkwarden Ilyth. You must say the passcode "Shadowtide" to Styria Olafur to avoid Aquilamar aggro.
- **Abyssal Lake** (24-33, Ripper's Keep): Dark water, Sivakayan zealots, and Nipper creatures. Darkwardens Awxa and Ealdred, then the Nipper King and Abyssal Lord. Secret entrance behind the waterfall in Silkengrass Meadowlands.
- **Fallen Braxonia** (28-33, Braxonian Desert): The buried ruins of Brax's city. Braxonian Honor Guards, Tempests, and Ascended. Prince Oslind and Prince Ardant are the final bosses. Braxonian Planar Guards (Fire and Ice variants) guard the inner chambers.
- **Prielian Cascade** (28-35, Windwashed Pass): The Priel bandit dynasty's stronghold. Priel Soldiers, Purists, Grimwatches, and Swiftblades. Tamed Malaroths serve as war beasts. Addison Priel, Julianna Priel, and the infamous Savannah Priel herself are the bosses. Failed Experiments and the Brood Mother are rare spawns.
- **Jaws of Sivakaya** (30-35, Duskenlight Coast): Requires an extensive unlock questline starting with A Hermit's Request. You must recover and light the Nighthollow Candle (the candle lit by Brax himself, given to Eldrin Shadowmire). A Memory of Sivakaya appears when you carry it. Then face the statue puzzle -- strafe around the room meeting the gaze of Sivakaya's rotating statue. Say "Azynthi" to the Ward of Piety to pass Aeiros. Bosses: Balthom and Aeiros (Soldiers of Sivakaya), Vessel Siraethe, and Sableheart, Steed of Sivakaya.
- **Secluded Sanctuary** (Level 35, Stowaway's Step): Accessed through a portal after completing the ritual questline. You face Shattered Sivakaya in conversation, then fight Shadow of Vitheo, Shadow of Brax, Shadow of Soluna, and Shadow of Fernalla simultaneously. Defeating all four spawns Azynthian Keeper, who drops the Eldrich Crystal needed to activate raid runes.

## Event Zones and Special Content

**Bellwain Island** (Any Level) is a puzzle zone that rewards Mooring Coins. No combat level requirement -- pure problem-solving. Captain Bellwain's story unfolds through environmental clues and key-finding. Bellsie Belwain offers context.

**Shivering Step** (Level 19-24) is a dream zone accessed through a mausoleum guarded by the Crypt Keeper. Three phases transform the zone as you progress through the questline involving Kio the Light Keeper, the Shivering Torch, and the Warming Flame. Boss encounters include Fearantula, Ghost of Plaxitheris (yes, the same serpent from Stowaway's Step, back in spectral form), and Mephallo. The **Shivering Tomb** within contains Dream and Warded Shadow through separate entrances.

**Reliquary Hall** (Level 16+) is the customizable guild hall in Port Azure. Defeat the Reliquary Fiend once to unlock full access. The hall supports 122 customization slots: raid rune portals, zone teleport runes, statue displays, and functional furniture including banks, auction houses, training dummies, vendors, and the Wisp Garden for proficiency respecs. Blueprints drop in Loomingwood, Rockshade Hold, and Azynthi's Garden. Direct furniture drops come from Fallen Braxonia, Blooming Sepulcher, Soluna's Landing, and Vitheo's Rest.

## The Divine Geography: Why the Land Is What It Is

Every zone in Erenshor carries the mark of the gods. The southern coasts where you begin are the furthest from the corruption -- the last places touched by the wars. Fernalla's Revival Plains bear the name of a goddess who died trying to stop her own corrupted mother. The great willow there, and the Blooming Sepulcher beneath it, are Fernalla's legacy.

The Braxonian Desert exists because Brax destroyed his own city to contain the Monolith of Corruption that Azynthi's ritual created. Fallen Braxonia is his grief made stone. The Blight is what happened where his containment failed -- Sivakaya's corruption spreading east like flowing mud.

Duskenlight Coast remembers when Sivakaya was good -- when she taught magic freely to mortals. The Jaws of Sivakaya, inside the giant skull, preserve memories of that lost benevolence. The Nighthollow Candle you carry there illuminates her past uncorrupted self.

Soluna's Landing is a literal impact crater. When the cosmic entity Soluna fell from the stars, she forcibly reshaped the Reavers, stripping their Void connection. Those who submitted became the first Paladins. Those who refused were consumed. Astra arrived to oppose Soluna, fought her in the heavens, and scattered into stardust at the crater's edge. The despair of the trapped Reavers fed Astra's rebirth.

Loomingwood is sanctuary. Warded by The Wisp against Sivakaya's corruption, the forest stands as proof that resistance is possible. Eldrin Shadowmire broke from Sivakaya's followers and retreated here, carrying the Nighthollow Candle that Brax lit for him.

Azynthi's Garden is where it all went wrong. A mortal sorcerer traveled north, touched the Void, returned changed, and offered Sivakaya power against Brax's warning. The ritual stripped her essence. The Monolith formed. And nearly five centuries of war, corruption, and divine grief followed.

The Secluded Sanctuary on Stowaway's Step brings it full circle. You face shadows of all four gods -- Vitheo, Brax, Soluna, Fernalla -- and then Azynthi's Keeper. The Shattered Sivakaya you meet there is what remains of the goddess who co-created the world. Whether she can be restored is the question the entire continent asks.

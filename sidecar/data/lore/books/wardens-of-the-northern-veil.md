# Wardens of the Northern Veil

> Source: `decompiled/project/AllBooks.cs` lines 36-43, key "ReaversOfTheNorth"
> Note: This is the longest book in the game at 32 pages. It details the origin of the Reavers and the arrival of Soluna and Astra.

## Page 1 (Title)

Wardens of the Northern Veil

## Page 2

There is little left to occupy the hours in what feel like our final days. We are sealed within the hills, prisoners of the beast that fell from the stars.

## Page 3

I suspect that anyone who finds these pages will dismiss them as tavern fiction. Still, I have nothing left but time, and so I write.

## Page 4

Our people are not Paladins. My brothers seem to be forgetting who they were, but for some cruel reason that mercy has not been given to me. I remember myself. I know what I am.

## Page 5

My ancestors came from the south of Erenshor like all others. But while Brax's attention lay elsewhere, Sivakaya took a group of our people by their hands, and she guided us quietly through forest and hill, urging silence as we went north, further than any had gone before.

## Page 6

She told us the veil between this world and the Void was thin, that darkness bled through unseen seams in this place. Our people were warned not to interfere. They were asked only to watch, to stand guard against the things that might crawl into being.

## Page 7

To further secure our mission, Sivakaya, in her teachings at the Duskenlight school where magic was first given freely to the world, spoke often of the North. She cautioned her students away from our place, saying wisdom lay in remaining south, far from the corruption that lingered there.

## Page 8

Living beside that power was never easy. Over generations, it shaped us in ways of which Sivakaya never warned. Our people first learned to endure it, then to coexist with it, and eventually they learned to draw strength from it.

## Page 9

They discovered that it could not be driven back or denied. Those who hungered for more than was offered were twisted into abominations and had to be destroyed. Those who declined its gift were driven mad. So we learned restraint. We accepted what came, never refused it, and never asked for more.

## Page 10

We built a life there. We watched the roads. We turned travelers away. And we became Reavers, guardians of the Darkness who protect the Darkness from the world, and the world from the Darkness.

## Page 11

But everything changed when the sorcerer arrived.

## Page 12

He claimed the southern schools had taught him all they could. He said the Duskenlight held no more answers, and he begged us to teach him the truths of the North. He said he was there with the blessing of Sivakaya herself.

## Page 13

He learned quickly, but patience never took root in him. The pace of the Void's offerings frustrated him. He wanted more than it would give, and when denied, he took it for himself.

## Page 14

One night he stepped through a door that would never open again. His body remained on Erenshor, but its insides were twisted and wrong.

## Page 15

He was what needed to be destroyed. He was what Sivakaya warned us might crawl from the seams of that Void. We failed.

## Page 16

The Void magnified his natural power, and he was far too strong to restrain. He left our city, and we never heard from him again.

## Page 17

Nearly five centuries passed. Our people continued on their mission of protection in silence.

## Page 18

Then the sky caught fire. The world shook. A mass of stone the size of a mountain was flung into the skies and fell southward in fire and ruin for days and days. We set out at once to reach Brax's people and Sivakaya's faithful to ensure they were intact.

## Page 19

Along the road we found camps of refugees who told of slaughter and loss, but none could explain the burning sky or falling stone. So we turned our expedition toward its origin.

## Page 20

That was when we met Soluna.

## Page 21

She loomed over us. Her physical presence was impressive, but standing alongside her was like standing within the heavens. We could sense the presence of knowledge that would not fit within our own minds.

## Page 22

Soluna sensed the divinity bound within us. She sensed the Void as well. Seeing an opportunity to gain a foothold on Erenshor, she bound us in her own power, reshaping us into something that served her design. The Void was torn from us and cast away by the force of the stars.

## Page 23

Her voice came as echoes. She told us we would claim this world in her name, that we would shepherd Erenshor into a golden age. She expected our thanks for her gift.

## Page 24

We refused. We did not thank her.

## Page 25

Our identities had already been stolen, and we would not give her our will as well. We swore we would never serve her, and that the truth of her domination would endure beyond us, and that her name would be forever known as one to be feared.

## Page 26

She answered with anger. Many of us fled into the mountains and hid there for a time.

## Page 27

Still, she called to us through the stars each night, promising love and mercy, promising release from this place. Those weak enough to listen wandered toward her light in the dark hours of night. Many were lost to us. Those she claimed carried her doctrine across the land, laying the foundations of the Paladins.

## Page 28

Those of us who remained prayed for her end.

## Page 29

It came at last. Astra arrived, and the heavens burned with war until Soluna withdrew. Astra, broken and spent, scattered herself into stardust along the crater's edge.

## Page 30

We believed hope had died with her. Instead, our despair fed Astra's rebirth. Any who left this place were drawn into her, consumed to restore what she had lost.

## Page 31

The guardians of Soluna did nothing to free us. We were a threat to the faith she left behind, and so we were allowed to rot in silence.

## Page 32

And so, this is the place where we are forever lost.
